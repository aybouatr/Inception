About

I'm a product designer who ended up specializing in the parts other designers don't want to touch: the tokens, the grid, the naming conventions, the seventeen shades of gray that need a reason to exist.

Most recently I led design systems at a fintech scale-up, taking three products with three different button styles down to one shared library used by 40 designers and engineers. Before that, I spent four years doing product design at a logistics startup, which is where I learned that a good system is really a form of kindness to your future self.

I work closely with engineering from day one — a system that only exists in Figma isn't a system, it's a mood board.

Process

STEP 1

Audit

Inventory every existing pattern, however inconsistent, before proposing a single new one.

STEP 2

Define

Set the tokens — color, type, space — as the shared vocabulary everything else is built from.

STEP 3

Build

Design and code components side by side, so the library is never just a picture of itself.

STEP 4

Document

Write usage guidelines a tired engineer at 5pm can actually follow.
